Instagram Strategy

Phase-based · milestone-driven · no fixed calendar

Guiding Principles

Show the real thing

No stock photos, no over-produced setups. Ponderosa's appeal is the place itself — let the space and the craft do the work.

Less is more

A smaller number of well-made posts beats a relentless feed. Posting when you have something worth saying builds more trust than filling a quota.

Document, don't perform

Treat Instagram as a visual journal of the build and the business, not a stage. This keeps it sustainable for someone who doesn't love social.

Local first

Central Point and Medford are the community. Prioritize content that feels like a neighborhood conversation, not a brand broadcast.

Content Pillars

Every post should fit one of these five buckets. They work in any phase.

Space The Place

Architecture, materials, light, details. The visual identity of the room itself.

  • Construction progress
  • Interior details / textures
  • Exterior / signage
  • Atmosphere shots
Craft The Work

Coffee preparation, sourcing, equipment — the tangible skill behind the product.

  • Espresso pulls
  • Bean sourcing / origin
  • Brewing methods
  • Behind the bar
People The Humans

The team, regulars, and the broader community that makes the shop real.

  • Staff introductions
  • Community partnerships
  • Regulars (with permission)
  • Events
Product The Menu

Drinks, food, seasonal offerings — what you actually sell.

  • Drink reveals
  • Food pairings
  • Seasonal specials
  • New additions
Story The Why

The origin, values, and vision behind Ponderosa — what makes it distinct from a chain.

  • Origin / founding story
  • Central Point roots
  • Design decisions
  • Longer-form captions

Phase Timeline

Phases advance by milestone, not calendar date. Move to the next phase when the trigger condition is met.

Phase 01 Construction Now → permit + build complete

Build the audience before you open. Show progress, build curiosity, establish the aesthetic. Low stakes — you're inviting people to watch something being made.

1–2× per week
  • Construction progress
  • Space reveals
  • Brand / design teases
  • Behind-the-scenes
  • Week one: introduce the account — one photo, one sentence about what's coming
  • Raw construction shots with a clean Ponderosa overlay or caption
  • Materials close-ups: tile, wood, hardware — the texture of the place
  • One Story Q&A: "What are you most excited to see here?"
  • Logo / brand identity reveal when ready
Phase 02 Pre-Open Build done → opening day

Ramp up frequency. Space is taking shape — start revealing the finished interior, menu direction, and the people behind the counter. Build anticipation deliberately.

3–4× per week
  • Finished space reveals
  • Staff intros
  • Menu teases
  • Soft countdown
  • "Meet the team" post for each staff member — one candid, one-line bio
  • First product photos: signature drink styled against the finished bar
  • Story countdown sticker when opening date is confirmed
  • Collab or shoutout to another local Central Point business
  • "Doors open [date]" announcement post with a strong image
Phase 03 Soft Open First day open → ~2 weeks

Document the first days honestly. Real customers, real reactions, the energy of a new thing. Don't over-produce it — raw and real performs well here and requires less effort.

Daily or near-daily
  • First-day moments
  • Customer reactions
  • Staff in action
  • Repost / reshare
  • Opening morning reel: 15–30 seconds, no music needed, let the ambient sound carry it
  • Reshare any customer tags — makes regulars feel seen early on
  • Quick "end of day" Story: what sold out, what surprised you
  • Staff candid mid-rush — no posed smiling required
Phase 04 Grand Opening When you throw the event

Treat this as a one-time campaign sprint. You have permission to post heavily for a week. Before, during, and after content tells a complete story.

Daily for ~1 week
  • Event announcement
  • Day-of coverage
  • Thank-you post
  • Community recap
  • Announcement post 1–2 weeks out: date, time, what to expect
  • Reminder Story the week of with event details
  • Live Stories during the event — low bar, just show what's happening
  • Next-day recap reel or carousel with the best moments
  • Gratitude post: "Thank you Central Point" closes the sprint gracefully
Phase 05 Steady State Post-grand opening, indefinitely

The sustainable rhythm. Rotate through the five content pillars. Three posts a week is enough — quality over consistency theater.

3× per week
  • Rotating pillars
  • Seasonal specials
  • Community moments
  • Stories for daily texture
  • One feed post per pillar per month keeps all five in rotation
  • Stories don't need to be polished — use them for daily specials, hours changes, casual moments
  • Seasonal drink launch = mini Phase 02 energy: tease, reveal, highlight
  • Once a quarter: a longer-form caption or origin story post
  • Resharing customer content is free content — encourage tagging with a subtle prompt at the counter

Voice on Instagram

Do

  • Write like a person, not a brand
  • Short captions usually beat long ones
  • Name the place — "Central Point" earns local credibility
  • Let good photography speak; caption can be minimal
  • Use first person: "we" for the shop, "I" sparingly for personal moments

Avoid

  • Hashtag walls (3–5 targeted ones max)
  • Corporate language ("we're excited to announce…")
  • Posting just to post — a quiet week is fine
  • Over-edited images that don't match the real space
  • Engagement bait ("comment below!")

Account Setup Checklist

  • Set up Instagram Business account Business account unlocks analytics and a contact button
  • Profile photo — wordmark or logomark, square crop Should read clearly at 40px
  • Bio — one line: what you are + where you are E.g. "Specialty coffee · Central Point, OR · Opening soon"
  • Link in bio — website or a Linktree with hours, location
  • Username — @ponderosacoffee or closest available handle Consistent with any future web presence
  • Post a first placeholder image before going public Avoid launching a profile that's completely empty
  • Connect to a Facebook Page (even if you don't post there) Required for Meta ads later; costs nothing now
