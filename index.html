Voice & Tone

Unhurried Short sentences, gentle pacing. Never push, never rush. Let the reader linger.

Writing Principles
01 Write at the pace of a pour Short sentences. White space. Let the reader breathe. If a sentence can be cut without losing meaning, cut it.
02 Name the place, name the thing Be specific. "Ethiopian natural process" beats "great beans." Specificity earns trust and signals care.
03 No hype words Avoid: amazing, perfect, best, love, obsessed, incredible, excited. These words cost credibility and add noise.
04 Lowercase where possible The brand name and logo live in lowercase. Follow that lead in copy — it feels softer and more approachable.
05 Let silence work Not every post needs a call to action. Sometimes the right word is no word at all — a photo, a dot, a pause.
06 Earn the emotion Don't tell people how to feel. Describe what's real — the smell, the morning light, the process — and trust the reader.
Copy Examples

Imagery

Coffee is the subject. Light is the style. People are the context.

Light Natural and directional. Morning and golden hour preferred. Soft shadows add depth — they're not a problem to fix. No flash, no harsh overhead.
Subject Coffee is the hero. Drinks, beans, pour process, the vessel. Close enough to see craft. Backgrounds are clean but lived-in — not sterile.
Human element Hands more than faces. A pour, a held cup, a gesture. The person implies warmth without becoming the portrait.
Mood Unhurried. Slight warmth in the tones. A photo that looks found rather than staged. Grain and imperfection are honest.
Do Steam rising from a ceramic cup · hands mid-pour · beans in a bag or tray · window light across a table · outdoor texture from the Rogue Valley
Avoid Stock photography · bright flat lighting · posed smiling portraits · cluttered or busy backgrounds · heavy filters or oversaturation

Guiding Principles
01 Document, don't perform Treat the feed as a visual journal of the build and the business — not a stage. This keeps it sustainable for someone who doesn't love social.
02 Show the real room No stock photos, no over-produced setups. Ponderosa's pull is the place itself. Let the space and the craft do the work.
03 Less is more Fewer well-made posts beat a relentless feed. Posting when you have something worth saying builds more trust than filling a quota.
04 Local before everything Central Point and the wider valley are the community. Prioritize content that reads like a neighborhood conversation, not a brand broadcast.
Content Pillars

Every post fits one of five buckets. They work in any phase.

Space The Place Architecture, materials, light, details. The visual identity of the room itself.
  • Construction progress
  • Interior details / textures
  • Exterior / signage
  • Atmosphere shots
Craft The Work Coffee preparation, sourcing, equipment — the tangible skill behind the product.
  • Espresso pulls
  • Bean sourcing / origin
  • Brewing methods
  • Behind the bar
People The Humans The team, regulars, and the broader community that makes the shop real.
  • Staff introductions
  • Community partnerships
  • Regulars (with permission)
  • Events
Product The Menu Drinks, food, seasonal offerings — what you actually sell.
  • Drink reveals
  • Food pairings
  • Seasonal specials
  • New additions
Story The Why The origin, values, and vision behind Ponderosa — what makes it distinct from a chain.
  • Origin / founding story
  • Central Point roots
  • Design decisions
  • Longer-form captions
Phase Timeline

Phases advance by milestone, not calendar date. Move forward when the trigger condition is met.

Phase 01 Construction Now → build complete
1–2× per week

Build the audience before you open. Show progress, build curiosity, establish the aesthetic. Low stakes — you're inviting people to watch something being made.

  • Construction progress
  • Space reveals
  • Brand teases
  • Behind-the-scenes
  • Week one: introduce the account — one photo, one sentence about what's coming.
  • Raw construction shots with a clean Ponderosa overlay or caption.
  • Materials close-ups: tile, wood, hardware — the texture of the place.
  • One Story Q&A: "what are you most excited to see here?"
  • Logo / brand identity reveal when ready.
Phase 02 Pre-Open Build done → opening day
3–4× per week

Ramp up frequency. Reveal the finished interior, menu direction, and the people behind the counter. Build anticipation deliberately.

  • Finished space
  • Staff intros
  • Menu teases
  • Soft countdown
  • "Meet the team" post for each staff member — one candid, one-line bio.
  • First product photos: signature drink styled against the finished bar.
  • Story countdown sticker when opening date is confirmed.
  • Collab or shoutout to a Central Point neighbor.
  • "Doors open [date]" announcement post with a strong image.
Phase 03 Soft Open First day → ~2 weeks
Daily or near-daily

Document the first days honestly. Real customers, real reactions, the energy of a new thing. Raw and real performs well here and requires less effort.

  • First-day moments
  • Customer reactions
  • Staff in action
  • Reshares
  • Opening morning reel: 15–30 seconds, no music needed, let the ambient sound carry it.
  • Reshare any customer tags — makes regulars feel seen early on.
  • Quick "end of day" Story: what sold out, what surprised you.
  • Staff candid mid-rush — no posed smiling required.
Phase 04 Grand Opening When you throw the event
Daily for ~1 week

A one-time campaign sprint. You have permission to post heavily for a week. Before, during, and after content tells a complete story.

  • Event announcement
  • Day-of coverage
  • Thank-you post
  • Community recap
  • Announcement post 1–2 weeks out: date, time, what to expect.
  • Reminder Story the week of with event details.
  • Live Stories during the event — low bar, just show what's happening.
  • Next-day recap reel or carousel with the best moments.
  • Gratitude post: "thank you, central point" closes the sprint gracefully.
Phase 05 Steady State Post-grand opening, indefinitely
3× per week

The sustainable rhythm. Rotate through the five pillars. Three posts a week is enough — quality over consistency theater.

  • Rotating pillars
  • Seasonal specials
  • Community moments
  • Stories for daily texture
  • One feed post per pillar per month keeps all five in rotation.
  • Stories don't need to be polished — use them for daily specials, hours changes, casual moments.
  • Seasonal drink launch = mini Phase 02 energy: tease, reveal, highlight.
  • Once a quarter: a longer-form caption or origin story post.
  • Resharing customer content is free content — a subtle prompt at the counter encourages tagging.

Caption Frameworks

Three reusable formulas. Pick the one that fits the image, then stop writing.

Observation One image. One sentence about what's there.

morning fog held over the valley all the way to ten.

Single Fact Name + origin or method. Nothing else.

washed ethiopian. mythical coffee, gilbert az.

Invitation Time, place, what's happening.

saturday, 9–noon. acoustic in the back room.

Geotag is more valuable than hashtags for a place-based business. Use five to seven tags total, never the full thirty.

Engagement Protocol
  • Comments — reply same day, in voice. Brief is the bar.
  • DMs — reply within 24 hours. Treat as customer service.
  • Tags & mentions — reshare to Story with a one-word thanks.
  • Critical or sensitive — reply once, kindly. Then take it offline.
What to Measure
  • Watch — saves, shares, profile visits, in-store mentions.
  • Ignore — follower count, raw like totals.
  • Cadence — review monthly. Don't read daily numbers.
  • Signal — a regular says "I saw your post." That's the metric.
The Rainy Tuesday Rule

Keep five to ten evergreen photos in reserve — a window light shot, a bean closeup, a signage detail, a quiet pour. A slow week should never feel empty, and you should never have to force a post.

  • One-line bio "specialty coffee · central point, OR · opening soon"
  • Link in bio Site or single landing page with hours, address, map.
  • Handle: @ponderosa_coffee Match the site and email domain wherever possible.
